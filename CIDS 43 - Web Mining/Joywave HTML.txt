<p><b>Joywave</b> is an American band from <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, formed in 2010. Their lineup consists of Daniel Armbruster (vocals), Joseph Morinelli (guitar), and Paul Brenner (drums). The band first became known for its collaboration with electronic music project <a href="/wiki/Big_Data_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Data (band)">Big Data</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Big_Data_song)" title="Dangerous (Big Data song)">Dangerous</a>". After releasing two EPs, their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/How_Do_You_Feel_Now%3F" title="How Do You Feel Now?">How Do You Feel Now?</a></i>, was released through their own Cultco Music label, an imprint of <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a>, in 2015. Their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Content_(Joywave_album)" title="Content (Joywave album)">Content</a></i>, was released on July 28, 2017. It was followed by their third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Possession_(Joywave_album)" title="Possession (Joywave album)">Possession</a></i>, which was released on March 13, 2020. Their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cleanse_(Joywave_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cleanse (Joywave album)">Cleanse</a></i>, was released on February 11, 2022. Their fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Permanent_Pleasure" title="Permanent Pleasure">Permanent Pleasure</a></i>, was released on May 17, 2024.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Early_years_(2010–2013)"><span id="Early_years_.282010.E2.80.932013.29"></span>Early years (2010–2013)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (2010–2013)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The members of Joywave all grew up in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-athatcher_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-athatcher-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Armbruster, Brenner, and Morinelli played together in various local bands growing up, including The Hoodies.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Joywave was officially formed in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-tsmith_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsmith-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Donnelly joined the band soon after, and they released their first mixtape, <i>77777</i>, in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-tsmith_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsmith-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2012, they released their first EP, <i>Koda Vista</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2013, they released their second mixtape, <i>88888</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/How_Do_You_Feel_Now%3F" title="How Do You Feel Now?">How Do You Feel Now?</a></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Joywave_Performing_in_2016.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Joywave_Performing_in_2016.jpg/250px-Joywave_Performing_in_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Joywave_Performing_in_2016.jpg/500px-Joywave_Performing_in_2016.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024" /></a><figcaption>Joywave performing in 2016</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 2012, the band was regularly traveling to perform in New York City, where Armbruster met and collaborated with producer Alan Wilkis on his new project <a href="/wiki/Big_Data_(music_project)" title="Big Data (music project)">Big Data</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Armbruster wrote four songs, including the single "Dangerous", which was recorded by Wilkis with Armbruster on vocals, and credited to Big Data featuring Joywave.<sup id="cite_ref-jones_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jones-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The song peaked at number two on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Rock_%26_Alternative_Airplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock &amp; Alternative Airplay">Rock &amp; Alternative Airplay</a> chart in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Following the viral success of "Dangerous", Joywave performed at <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> in Chicago<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and made their television debut in July 2014 on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Seth_Meyers" title="Late Night with Seth Meyers">Late Night with Seth Meyers</a></i>, where they performed the song with Big Data, as well as their own song "<a href="/wiki/Tongues_(song)" title="Tongues (song)">Tongues</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ebloch_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebloch-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 2015, they would also perform on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live" class="mw-redirect" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live">Jimmy Kimmel Live</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Morning_Buzz_Live" title="Big Morning Buzz Live">Big Morning Buzz Live</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The band then signed a contract with the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>-owned label <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a> to form their own imprint, Cultco,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and obtained unique permission to use samples from Disney's movie catalog in their music.<sup id="cite_ref-rgokhman_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgokhman-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The band released their second EP, <i>How Do You Feel?</i>, later in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-athatcher_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-athatcher-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>Joywave's debut LP, <i><a href="/wiki/How_Do_You_Feel_Now%3F" title="How Do You Feel Now?">How Do You Feel Now?</a></i>, was released on April 21, 2015 in the US and June 22, 2015 in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The album was a compilation of tracks composed by the band's members over the preceding decade.<sup id="cite_ref-ssweeney_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssweeney-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It reached #25 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Alternative Albums">Alternative Albums</a> chart, #34 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Rock_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Rock Albums">Top Rock Albums</a> chart, and #3 on the <a href="/wiki/Heatseekers_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Heatseekers Albums">Heatseekers Albums</a> chart. Four singles were released from the album: "<a href="/wiki/Somebody_New_(Joywave_song)" title="Somebody New (Joywave song)">Somebody New</a>", "Tongues", "<a href="/wiki/Destruction_(song)" title="Destruction (song)">Destruction</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Now_(Joywave_song)" title="Now (Joywave song)">Now</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> A music video for "Tongues" was directed by the duo <a href="/wiki/Daniels_(directors)" title="Daniels (directors)">Daniels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The band toured in support of the album for seven months from April to October,<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> including segments opening shows for <a href="/wiki/The_Kooks" title="The Kooks">The Kooks</a> in North America<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and for <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Flowers" title="Brandon Flowers">Brandon Flowers</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Killers" title="The Killers">The Killers</a> in the United Kingdom,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> where they also played <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Festival">Leeds Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Joywave released <i><a href="/wiki/Swish_(album)" title="Swish (album)">Swish</a></i>, a novelty record which only featured nine copies of the song "Destruction" and a B-side track titled "Life in a Bubble I Blew".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The title is a reference to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s 2016 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Pablo" title="The Life of Pablo">The Life of Pablo</a></i>, which was originally going to be titled <i>Swish</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The band also went on a five-week US tour with <a href="/wiki/Metric_(band)" title="Metric (band)">Metric</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Content_(2017–2018)"><span id="Content_.282017.E2.80.932018.29"></span><i>Content</i> (2017–2018)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Content (2017–2018)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Content_(Joywave_album)" title="Content (Joywave album)">Content (Joywave album)</a></div>
<p>Joywave began recording their next LP in late 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-rledonne_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rledonne-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Now working with a larger budget, they relocated their home studio in Rochester to a barn in <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield,_New_York" title="Bloomfield, New York">Bloomfield, New York</a> and recorded the album <i><a href="/wiki/Content_(Joywave_album)" title="Content (Joywave album)">Content</a></i> over the course of four months.<sup id="cite_ref-ssweeney_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssweeney-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Five singles were released ahead of the album,<sup id="cite_ref-ssweeney_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ssweeney-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> including the song "Content" in April 2017<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and "It's a Trip!" in May.<sup id="cite_ref-lhoward_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lhoward-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>Content</i> was released on July 28, following a launch party held at Rochester's <a href="/wiki/First_Federal_Plaza" title="First Federal Plaza">First Federal Plaza</a>. An injury prevented Donnelly from touring for the album,<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak2-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and he departed from the band later in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In the summer of 2017, they toured across the United States with <a href="/wiki/Young_the_Giant" title="Young the Giant">Young the Giant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cold_War_Kids" title="Cold War Kids">Cold War Kids</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lhoward_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lhoward-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In November 2017, the band announced the second leg of their <i>Thanks. Thanks for Coming</i> tour, which began in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> on February 10, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The band appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Show_with_Stephen_Colbert" title="The Late Show with Stephen Colbert">The Late Show with Stephen Colbert</a></i> on February 9, 2018, where they performed the single "Doubt".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They toured with <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars" title="Thirty Seconds to Mars">Thirty Seconds to Mars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walk_the_Moon" title="Walk the Moon">Walk the Moon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MisterWives" title="MisterWives">MisterWives</a> on Thirty Seconds to Mars's North American tour in the summer of 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in September 2018 they toured with <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Briggs" title="Bishop Briggs">Bishop Briggs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Possession_(2018–2020)"><span id="Possession_.282018.E2.80.932020.29"></span><i>Possession</i> (2018–2020)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Possession (2018–2020)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Possession_(Joywave_album)" title="Possession (Joywave album)">Possession (Joywave album)</a></div>
<p>Joywave returned to the extensive use of samples on their third LP, <i>Possession</i>, primarily sourced from the recordings on the <a href="/wiki/Voyager_Golden_Record" title="Voyager Golden Record">Voyager Golden Record</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak3_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak3-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Five of the album's twelve tracks were released ahead of the album,<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak3_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak3-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> including the singles "Like a Kennedy", released on June 21, 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "Obsession", released on August 9, 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and "Half Your Age", released on January 7, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Despite the extensive promotion for the album, its release on March 13, 2020 was overshadowed by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Joywave performed their last live show of 2020 on March 1 in Berlin, Germany, before returning home to Rochester, where a state of emergency was declared on March 14.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The planned Possession Tour was delayed repeatedly and ultimately canceled in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Cleanse_(2021–2024)"><span id="Cleanse_.282021.E2.80.932024.29"></span><i>Cleanse</i> (2021–2024)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cleanse (2021–2024)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cleanse_(Joywave_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cleanse (Joywave album)">Cleanse (Joywave album)</a></div>
<p>During the period of COVID-related restrictions in 2020 and 2021, Armbruster resumed writing and composition for another album, writing ten tracks of reflections on the pandemic which would form the album <i><a href="/wiki/Cleanse_(album)" title="Cleanse (album)">Cleanse</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Armbruster handled production in his home studio with contributions from Brenner and Morinelli shared over the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak4_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak4-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Keyboardist Benjamin Bailey departed from the band in 2021 to work on solo projects.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak4_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak4-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Joywave released the four-track EP <i>Every Window is a Mirror</i> on June 25, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> An additional single, "Cyn City 2000", was released on November 2. <i>Cleanse</i> was released on February 11, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In October 2021, the band announced The Cleanse Tour, which began in <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Harrisburg, Pennsylvania">Harrisburg, Pennsylvania</a> on February 26, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Bassist Kevin Mahoney joined as a touring musician, as well as keyboardists Connor Ehman and Jason Milton.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak4_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak4-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In the fall of 2022, Joywave coheadlined The Welcome to Hellvetica Tour with <a href="/wiki/I_Dont_Know_How_But_They_Found_Me" title="I Dont Know How But They Found Me">I Dont Know How But They Found Me</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In August 2022, Joywave announced a UK and EU tour, which began in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> on November 1, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In December of 2022, they announced another tour of the United States called <i>Express Wash,</i> which took place in March and April 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In Spring 2022, the band recorded a version of the <i><a href="/wiki/NHL_on_ESPN" title="NHL on ESPN">NHL on ESPN</a></i> theme for playoff broadcasts.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On June 24, 2022, the band announced a <a href="/wiki/Live_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Live album">live album</a> titled <i>Live</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The album was recorded from multiple shows on the Cleanse Tour and released on July 29, 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The band contributed 3 songs to the soundtrack of the 2023 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Jedi:_Survivor" title="Star Wars Jedi: Survivor">Star Wars Jedi: Survivor</a></i>, credited as "Yubnib Zekk and the Main Characters".<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Permanent_Pleasure_(2024-present)"><span id="Permanent_Pleasure_.282024-present.29"></span><i>Permanent Pleasure</i> (2024-present)</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Permanent Pleasure (2024-present)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Pleasure" title="Permanent Pleasure">Permanent Pleasure</a></div>
<p>On February 16, 2024, Joywave released the single "Brain Damage".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>54<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On March 22, 2024, they released the single "Scared", and announced that their upcoming album <i><a href="/wiki/Permanent_Pleasure" title="Permanent Pleasure">Permanent Pleasure</a></i> was scheduled for release on May 17, 2024.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In May 2024, the band announced the Permanent Pleasure World Tour, which began on September 11, 2024, in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and concluded on November 14, 2024, in <a href="/wiki/Utrecht" title="Utrecht">Utrecht</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On January 27, 2025, the band announced their Here to Perform... tour, which will begin on April 16, 2025, in New Haven, Connecticut, running through May, with <a href="/wiki/Little_Image" title="Little Image">Little Image</a> as their supporting act.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Musical_styles_and_influences">Musical styles and influences</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joywave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Musical styles and influences"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Joywave's music has been described as <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-athatcher_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-athatcher-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-rledonne_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rledonne-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Indietronica" class="mw-redirect" title="Indietronica">indietronica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-popmatters_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-popmatters-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Critics have noted influences from pop and synth rock acts of previous decades, including <a href="/wiki/Tears_for_Fears" title="Tears for Fears">Tears for Fears</a> and <a href="/wiki/LCD_Soundsystem" title="LCD Soundsystem">LCD Soundsystem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-popmatters_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-popmatters-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The album <i>Content</i> drew comparisons to the band <a href="/wiki/Hot_Chip" title="Hot Chip">Hot Chip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> According to Donnelly, the Rochester music scene largely consisted of metal bands at the time of Joywave's formation, which influenced their sound to be more aggressive and edgy on <i>How Do You Feel Now?</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rgokhman_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rgokhman-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Joywave have paid homage to their hometown of Rochester on several projects. Armbruster has said of his hometown pride, "I think it’s a shame so many people from here move to another city and never even acknowledge they’re from Rochester". Press badges for their 2015 world tour included a copy of the logo for <a href="/wiki/Kodak" title="Kodak">Kodak</a>, where parents of all five band members had worked.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later that year, the band jokingly attempted to crowdfund a purchase of the abandoned local <a href="/wiki/Medley_Center_Mall" class="mw-redirect" title="Medley Center Mall">Medley Center Mall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jspevak_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jspevak-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The album <i>Permanent Pleasure</i> samples a message by former mayor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_P._Ryan_Jr." title="Thomas P. Ryan Jr.">Thomas P. Ryan Jr.</a> and the song "Rochester is a Grand Old City" from a 1984 album commemorating the 150th anniversary of the city's founding.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>59<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
